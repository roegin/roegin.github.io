Obviously, the real issue has nothing to do with the fear itself, but, rather, how we hold the fear. For some, the fear is totally irrelevant. For others, it creates a state of paralysis. The former hold their fear from a position of power (choice, energy, and action), and the latter hold it from a position of pain (helplessness, depression, and paralysis).
显然，真正的问题与恐惧本身无关，而是我们如何控制恐惧。对一些人来说，恐惧是完全无关紧要的。对另一些人来说，它会造成一种瘫痪状态。前者从权力的位置(选择、能量和行动)控制他们的恐惧，后者从痛苦的位置(无助、沮丧和瘫痪)控制它。

From this it can be seen that the secret in handling fear is to move yourself from a position of pain to a position of power. The fact that you have the fear then becomes irrelevant.
由此可以看出，处理恐惧的秘诀在于将自己从痛苦的位置转移到强大的位置。你有恐惧的事实就变得无关紧要了。

Let’s talk about the word “power.” Some people say that they do not like the concept of power and want no part of it. It is true that in our world the word “power” has some negative overtones. It often implies control over others, and, unfortunately, is often misused.
我们来谈谈”权力”这个词吧有些人说他们不喜欢权力的概念，也不想参与其中。的确，在我们的世界里，“权力”这个词有一些负面的含义。它经常意味着对他人的控制，不幸的是，它经常被滥用。

The kind of power I am talking about is entirely different. In fact, it makes you less manipulative of those around you, and certainly more loving. I am talking about power within the self. This means power over your perceptions of the world, power over how you react to situations in your life, power to do what is necessary for your own self-growth, power to create joy and satisfaction in your life, power to act, and power to love.
我所说的这种权力是完全不同的。事实上，它让你不再那么控制你周围的人，而是更有爱心。我说的是自我的力量。这意味着掌控你对世界的感知，掌控你如何应对你生活中的情况，掌控你自我成长所需要的做什么的权力，掌控你生活中创造快乐和满足的权力，掌控行动的权力，掌控爱的权力。

This kind of power has nothing to do with anyone else. It is not egomania, but a healthy self-love. In fact, egomaniacs have absolutely no feeling of power—thus their compelling need to control those around them. Their lack of power leaves them in a perpetual state of fear, since their survival depends on the outside world. No one is more unloving than a person who can’t own his or her own power. Such people spend their lives trying to pull it out of everyone else. Their need creates all sorts of manipulative behavior.
这种力量与其他人无关。这不是自大，而是一种健康的自爱。事实上，自大狂完全没有权力感，因此他们迫切需要控制身边的人。他们缺乏权力使他们永远处于恐惧状态，因为他们的生存依赖于外部世界。没有人比一个不能拥有自己权力的人更没有爱心。这样的人一辈子都在试图从别人那里得到爱。他们的需求产生了各种各样的操纵行为。

The kind of power I’m talking about leaves you free, since you don’t expect the rest of the world to fill you up. It’s not the ability to get someone else to do what you want them to do. It’s the ability to get yourself to do what you want to do. If you do not own this kind of power, you lose your sense of peace. You are in a very vulnerable place.
我说的这种力量能让你自由，因为你不指望世界其他地方能填满你。它不是让别人做你想让他们做的事的能力。而是让自己做自己想做的事情的能力。如果你没有这种力量，你就会失去平静的感觉。你处在一个非常脆弱的地方。

I have found that women are more put off than men are by the concept of power, for obvious reasons. Men have been conditioned to believe that to be powerful is good. Women have been conditioned to believe that to be powerful is unfeminine and unattractive. It is my experience that nothing could be further from the truth.
我发现女性比男性更容易被权力的概念所拖累，原因显而易见。男人已经习惯于相信强大是件好事。女人已经习惯于认为强大是没有女人味和吸引力的。根据我的经验，事实并非如此。

A self-assured woman who is in control of her life draws like a magnet. She is so filled with positive energy that people want to be around her. Yet it is only when she has become powerful within herself that she can become authentic and loving to those around her. The truth is that love and power go together. With power, one can really begin to open the heart. With no power, love is distorted.
一个自信的女人掌控着自己的生活，她的吸引力就像一块磁铁。她充满了积极的能量，人们都想和她在一起。然而，只有当她内心变得强大时，她才能变得真实，对周围的人充满爱。事实上，爱和力量是相辅相成的。有了力量，一个人才能真正开始打开心扉。没有力量，爱就会被扭曲。

For the women reading this book, a good antidote to any inner conflict between power and femininity is to repeat to yourself at least twenty-five times each morning, noon, and night:
对于读这本书的女性来说，解决权力和女性气质之间内心冲突的一个好办法就是每天早上、中午和晚上至少对自己重复25次:

I AM POWERFUL AND I AM LOVED.
I AM POWERFUL AND i AM LOVED 我很强大，我被爱着。

还有:

I AM POWERFUL AND I AM LOVING.
我充满力量，我充满爱。

An energizing variation is:
一个令人振奋的变化是:

I AM POWERFUL AND I LOVE IT!
我是强大的，我爱它！

Say these three statements aloud right now. Feel the energy the words convey. Their constant repetition will help make the concepts of power and love more compatible and certainly more comfortable.
现在大声说出这三句话。感受这些话所传达的能量。它们的不断重复将有助于使权力和爱的概念更加兼容，当然也更加舒适。

Now that I’ve explained the kind of power I’m talking about, let’s explore how to use the Pain-to-Power concept in daily life.
现在我已经解释了我所说的力量的类型，让我们来探索如何在日常生活中使用痛苦到力量的概念。

第一步是创建一个疼痛-电力图表，如下所示:

As we look at the Pain-to-Power continuum, most of us can place ourselves somewhere in the middle of the chart. We’re not totally incapacitated by our fears, but we’re not exactly feeling a great sense of power and excitement, nor are we quickly sprinting to our goals. We seem to be taking the arduous route over the mountain carrying two suitcases and a watermelon rather than flying on the wings of eagles. As another ancient sage once said, “The pathway is smooth. Why do you throw rocks before you?”
当我们观察痛苦到能量的连续性时，我们大多数人可以将自己置于图表的中间位置。我们并没有完全被恐惧所束缚，但我们并没有感受到巨大的力量和兴奋，也没有迅速奔向我们的目标。我们似乎是带着两个行李箱和一个西瓜艰难地翻山越岭，而不是骑着鹰的翅膀飞行。正如另一位古代圣人所说: “这条路很平坦。你为什么在你面前扔石头?”

Using the Pain-to-Power Chart as a frame of reference, you can begin to clear the rocks in front of you. The following steps will help in the clearing process:
使用疼痛到权力图表作为一个参考框架，你可以开始清除你面前的岩石。下面的步骤将有助于清理过程:

1. Draw an enlargement of the Pain-to-Power Chart and place it on your wall. Just the simple act of making the enlargement will make you feel a little more powerful. You are already taking action! Remember that much of the trick of moving from pain to power is taking action. ACTION IS VERY POWERFUL! Once the chart is on your wall it will serve as a constant reminder of where you want to go in life—from pain to power. Awareness is half the battle. Having the chart physically present will also help you motivate yourself to keep moving in the right direction.
1.画一张放大的疼痛感图表，并把它放在你的墙上。仅仅是简单的放大就会让你感觉更有力量。你已经在行动了！记住，从痛苦走向权力的诀窍就是采取行动。行动是非常强大的！一旦你把图表贴在墙上，它就会不断提醒你人生的目标——从痛苦到力量。意识是成功的一半。把图表放在身边也能帮助你激励自己朝着正确的方向前进。

2. Just to keep you from taking yourself too seriously, you might want to write somewhere on your chart “Angels fly because they take themselves lightly.” I heard this quote by Gilbert K. Chesterton a long time ago, and it still makes me smile. It constantly reminds me that you can drop an awful lot of excess baggage if you learn to play with life instead of fight it.
2.为了不让你把自己看得太严重，你可能想在你的图表上写上“天使飞翔是因为他们轻视自己。”很久以前，我听到吉尔伯特 · k · 切斯特顿(gilbert k。 chesterton)说过这句话，至今仍让我微笑。它不断地提醒我，如果你学会与生活打交道，而不是与之抗争，你可以卸下许多可怕的过重的包袱。

3. Put a pin at the place on the chart where you see yourself situated at this moment in your life. Are you in the middle, where you sometimes feel depressed and paralyzed and at other times feel more in control? Or do you definitely find yourself on the far left side, where there is little you are able to do to pull yourself out of the rut? Or perhaps you are already on the right side, where most of the time you feel you are really moving ahead with your life, with only a few areas that need to be worked on. I doubt that anyone reading this book has reached their goal of attaining absolute power over the self. Even the Buddhas have their days! There are always new experiences that challenge a sense of personal power.
3.在图表上你看到自己此刻所处的位置放一个大头针。你是否处在中间，有时感到沮丧和瘫痪，有时感到更多的控制？或者你肯定发现自己处于极左的一边，在那里你几乎无法让自己摆脱常规？或者也许你已经站在了正确的一边，大多数时候你觉得你的生活真的在前进，只有少数几个方面需要改进。我怀疑读这本书的人是否已经达到了他们获得超越自我的绝对权力的目标。即使佛陀也有他们的日子！总有新的体验挑战个人力量的感觉。

4. Each day look at the chart and ask yourself, “Do I see myself at the same place, or have I moved?” Move the pin accordingly.
4.每天看着图表，问问自己: “我是在同一个地方看到自己，还是我已经移动了?”相应地移动别针。

5. If you keep in mind the direction you want to go, it will help you make decisions about what you are doing in your life. Before you take any action in life, ask yourself: “Is this action moving me to a more powerful place?” If it isn’t, you will think twice about doing it. A word of caution: If you go ahead any way, knowing the action will keep you in a position of pain, don’t get angry with yourself about it. Just notice where you are not taking responsibility. The next time, you can make a different decision. Use your “mistakes” as learning experiences. Remember that each time you get angry at yourself for an action you have taken, you keep yourself on the side of pain.
5.如果你牢记你想要走的方向，它将帮助你决定你在生活中做什么。在你采取任何行动之前，问问你自己: “这个行动是不是把我带到了一个更强大的地方?”如果不是，你会三思而后行。提醒一句: 如果你继续下去，知道这个行动会让你处于痛苦的境地，不要为此生自己的气。只要注意你没有承担责任的地方。下一次，你可以做一个不同的决定。利用你的“错误”作为学习的经验。记住，每当你因为自己的行为而生自己的气时，你就让自己站在痛苦的一边。

6. Make your use of the chart fun. Having it as a game keeps you light about the situation. If you have children, they can create their own charts, and you can make a family game out of the experience of growing.
6.让你对图表的使用变得有趣。把它当做一个游戏，可以让你对情况了如指掌。如果你有孩子，他们可以创建他们自己的图表，你可以根据成长的经验制作一个家庭游戏。

7. You might want to make different charts for different areas of your life. To be really powerful, you need to be in charge of all aspects of your life—your work, relationships, environment, body, and so on. Often people are very powerful in some parts of their lives and pathetic in others. For example, I am very powerful in terms of my career, but need to work on the area of exercise.
7.你可能想为你生活的不同领域制作不同的图表。要真正变得强大，你需要掌控你生活的方方面面——你的工作、人际关系、环境、身体等等。通常人们在他们生活的某些方面非常强大，而在其他方面却很可悲。例如，我在我的职业生涯中非常强大，但是我需要在运动领域工作。

Note that your movement on the chart is determined only by your own intuitive sense of how far you are progressing in gaining more power in your life. No one else can judge that, though they may try. Although your life may look exactly the same to the outside world, it is your own sense of internal peace and growth that determines where you are on the chart. It is, totally, a feeling within.
请注意，你在图表上的运动只是由你自己的直觉决定的，你在获得更多的权力在你的生活中有多远的进步。没有其他人可以判断这一点，尽管他们可能会尝试。虽然你的生活可能看起来完全相同的外部世界，它是你自己的内心平静和成长的感觉，决定你在图表上的位置。它完全是一种内在的感觉。

You may wonder if you really need to go to such lengths to get yourself moving. Trust me—you do! In the beginning, you need all the gimmicks you can get to remind you of where you want to go. You don’t become powerful without concentrating on power. As you must have figured out by now, simply knowing what to do does not mean that you do it, or, for that matter, even remember it.
你可能想知道你是否真的需要走这么远才能让自己动起来。相信我——你确实需要！在开始的时候，你需要所有的噱头来提醒你你想去哪里。如果你不专注于权力，你就不会变得强大。正如你现在一定已经明白的那样，仅仅知道要做什么并不意味着你要去做，或者，就此而言，甚至记住它。

To help you on your pain-to-power path, it’s important that you begin to develop a Pain-to-Power Vocabulary. The way you use words has a tremendous impact on the quality of your life. Certain words are destructive; others are empowering. Choose to move to a Pain-to-Power Vocabulary as follows:
为了帮助你走上痛苦到力量的道路，重要的是你要开始建立痛苦到力量的词汇表。你使用词汇的方式对你的生活质量有着巨大的影响。某些词语具有破坏性，而另一些则具有授权性。选择一个“痛苦到力量”的词汇，如下:

“I can’t” implies you have no control over your life, whereas “I won’t” puts a situation in the realm of choice. From this moment on, strike “I can’t” from your vocabulary. When you give your subconscious the message “I can’t,” your subconscious really believes you and registers on its computer: WEAK . . . WEAK . . . WEAK. Your subconscious believes only what it hears, not what is true. You might be saying “I can’t” simply to get out of a dinner invitation—such as, “I can’t come to dinner tonight. I have to prepare for tomorrow’s meeting,” but your subconscious is registering, “He’s weak!” In fact, “I can’t come to dinner” is an untruth. The truth is “I can come to dinner, but I am choosing to do something that has a higher priority at the moment.” But the subconscious can’t discern the difference and is still registering “weak.”
“ i can’t”意味着你无法控制自己的生活，而“ i won’t”意味着你可以选择某种情况。从现在开始，把“我不能”从你的字典里删掉。当你向你的潜意识传达“我不能”的信息时，你的潜意识真的相信你，并且在它的电脑上注册: 弱，弱，弱。你的潜意识只相信它听到的，而不相信真相。你可能说“我不能”仅仅是为了逃避一个晚餐邀请——比如，“我今晚不能来吃晚餐。我必须为明天的会议做准备,”但是你的潜意识正在记录，“他太弱了!”事实上，“我不能来吃晚饭”是一个谎言。事实是“我可以来吃晚饭，但我现在选择做一些更重要的事情。”但是潜意识无法分辨其中的区别，并且仍然记录着“软弱”

Although you may want to be more delicate to your host than to utter the above statement, you can still stay away from the words “I can’t.” “I’d love to come to dinner, but I have a meeting tomorrow that’s important to me. I’ll feel better walking in totally prepared. So I’ll pass for tonight and hope you’ll invite me again.” That statement has truth, integrity, and power. The subconscious hears you stating your priorities with clarity and choosing the outcome that serves your own growth. Choosing this way doesn’t leave you the helpless victim of your meeting.
尽管你可能希望对你的主人更加谨慎，而不是说出上面的话，但是你仍然可以远离“我不能”这个词”我很乐意参加晚宴，但我明天有个重要的会议。完全准备好走进去，我会感觉更好。所以我今晚就不去了，希望你能再次邀请我。”这句话有真实性，正直性和力量。潜意识听到你清晰地陈述你的优先事项，并选择有利于你自身成长的结果。选择这种方式不会让你成为会议的无助受害者。

“I should” is another loser. It, too, implies that you have no choices in life. “I could” is more powerful. “I could visit my mother, but I’m choosing to go to the movies today.” This puts things in the realm of choice instead of obligation. “I can visit my mother or I can go to the movies. I think I’ll choose my mother today.” “Shoulds” bring on guilt and upset—totally draining emotions. Your power is taken away every time you utter the words “I should.”
“我应该”是另一个失败者。这也意味着你在生活中没有选择。“我可以”更有力量。“我可以去看我妈妈，但我今天选择去看电影。”这使得事情处于选择的范围而不是义务。我可以去看我妈妈或者去看电影。我想今天我会选择我的母亲。”“应该”会带来内疚和沮丧——完全消耗情绪。每当你说出“我应该”这个词的时候，你的力量就会消失

“It’s not my fault” is another beauty. Once again, you look helpless. It’s better to take responsibility for whatever happens to you in life than to always be the victim. “It’s not my fault I got sick”; “It’s not my fault I lost the job.” If you are willing to take responsibility, then you might see what you can change in the future. Relative to illness, say, “I’m totally responsible for my illness. Let’s see what I can do to prevent it from happening again. I can change my diet. I can reduce stress. I can stop smoking. I can get enough sleep.” And so on. Watch how powerful you become! The same occurs with the lost job. If you are responsible, you can be better prepared the next time; you can find out what made the difference. You are in control. Each time you find yourself in better control of your life you are moving to a position of power, which will ultimately reduce your fear level.
“这不是我的错”是另一个美丽。又一次，你看起来很无助。与其一直做受害者，不如为生活中发生的任何事情负责。生病不是我的错，丢了工作也不是我的错如果你愿意承担责任，那么你可能会看到未来你可以改变什么。对于疾病，你可以说: “我对我的疾病负全部责任。让我们看看我能做些什么来防止它再次发生。我可以改变我的饮食。我可以减少压力。我可以戒烟。我可以得到足够的睡眠。”诸如此类。看看你变得多么强大！丢掉工作也是如此。如果你负有责任，你可以在下一次做更好的准备; 你可以找出是什么造成了这种差异。你在掌控之中。每次当你发现自己能更好地控制自己的生活时，你就在向一个有权力的位置移动，这将最终降低你的恐惧水平。

“It’s a problem” is another deadening phrase. It’s heavy and negative. “It’s an opportunity” opens the door to growth. Each time you can see the gift in life’s obstacles, you can handle difficult situations in a rewarding way. Each time you have the opportunity to stretch your capacity to handle the world, the more powerful you become.
另一个令人丧气的短语是“这是个问题”。它沉重而消极。“这是一个机会”打开了增长的大门。每当你看到生活中的障碍时，你就能以一种有益的方式处理困难的情况。每次你有机会拓展你处理世界的能力，你就会变得更加强大。

“I hope” is another victim’s phrase. “I know” has far more power.
“我希望”是另一个受害者的说法，“我知道”更有力量。

I hope I will get a job.
我希望我能找到一份工作。

I know I will get a job.
我知道我会找到工作的。

What a difference! The first sets you up for worry and sleepless nights. The second has peace and calm about it.
多么不同啊！第一个让你陷入焦虑和失眠的夜晚。第二种是平和和平静。

“If only” is boring. You can hear the whine behind it. “Next time” implies that you have learned from the situation and will put the learning to use another time. For example, “If only I hadn’t said that to Tom” can be restated, “I’ve learned Tom is sensitive about this issue. Next time, I’ll be more sensitive.”
”要是”太无聊了。你可以听到背后的抱怨。“下一次”意味着你已经从这种情况中学到了东西，并将把这种学习用在下一次。例如，“如果我没有对汤姆说这些”可以重申，“我知道汤姆对这个问题很敏感。下一次，我会更加敏感。”

“What will I do?” Again you can hear the whine and the fear implied in these words. You, like everyone else, have incredible sources of power within that you haven’t used before. It would serve you to say to yourself, “I know I’ll handle it. I have nothing to worry about.” Instead of, “I’ve lost my job! What will I do?” try “I’ve lost my job. I know I’ll handle it.”
“我该怎么办?”你可以再一次听到这些话语中隐含的抱怨和恐惧。你和其他人一样，拥有难以置信的力量源泉，这是你以前从未使用过的。你可以对自己说，“我知道我会处理好的。我没什么好担心的而不是“我丢了工作！我该怎么办?”尝试“我失业了。我知道我会处理好的。”

“It’s terrible” is bandied around in the most inappropriate circumstances. For example, “I lost my wallet. Isn’t that terrible?” What’s so terrible about losing a wallet? It’s certainly an inconvenience; it’s hardly terrible. “I gained two pounds. Isn’t that terrible?” It’s hardly terrible to gain two pounds. Yet that’s the way we talk about trivia in our lives. And our subconscious is registering, DISASTER . . . DISASTER . . . DISASTER. Replace “it’s terrible” with “It’s a learning experience.”
“太可怕了”在最不恰当的情况下被四处传播。例如，“我的钱包丢了。是不是很可怕?”丢了钱包有什么可怕的？这当然是一种不便，但并不可怕。我胖了两磅。是不是很糟糕?”体重增加两磅并不可怕。然而，这就是我们谈论生活琐事的方式。我们的潜意识正在记录，灾难，灾难，灾难。用“这是一次学习的经历”代替“糟糕透顶”

While you might feel more justified in saying “It’s terrible” if a loved one has cancer, keep in mind that this attitude takes away your power to deal with the situation. There are many who have learned important things from the experience. I know, because I am one of those people.
如果你所爱的人得了癌症，你可能会觉得说“这太可怕了”更有道理，但请记住，这种态度会让你失去处理这种情况的能力。很多人从这段经历中学到了很多重要的东西。我知道，因为我就是其中之一。

My experience of cancer taught me many wonderful things about myself and the people around me. Most important, I learned how much I was loved. I saw a tender side of my fiancé, now my husband, that I had never seen before, and our love deepened immeasurably. We stopped taking each other for granted. Also, I have changed my life in many positive ways. I’ve become a much more aware eater. I’ve learned how to eliminate the anger, resentment, and stress that was very much a part of my daily life prior to my illness. My cancer experience has given my husband and me an opportunity to contribute something to this world. I wrote a very positive article about my mastectomy, which I know has been of value to many men and women. My husband and I have appeared on television together to relate our experience, bringing reassurance to viewers. So you see, cancer can be a great learning experience and an opportunity to give.
我的癌症经历教会了我很多关于自己和周围人的美好事物。最重要的是，我知道了我是多么的被爱着。我看到了我的未婚夫，现在是我的丈夫，我以前从未见过的温柔的一面，我们的爱无限加深。我们不再认为彼此是理所当然的。同时，我也在许多积极的方面改变了我的生活。我变成了一个更有意识的饮食者。我已经学会了如何消除愤怒、怨恨和压力，在我生病之前，这些都是我日常生活的一部分。我的癌症经历给了我和我的丈夫一个为这个世界做出贡献的机会。我写了一篇关于我的乳房切除术的非常积极的文章，我知道这对很多男人和女人都有价值。我丈夫和我一起在电视上讲述我们的经历，给观众带来安慰。所以你看，癌症可以是一个很好的学习经历，也是一个给予的机会。

You get the picture. Begin eliminating the terribles, can’ts, problems, struggles, and so on from your vocabulary. Maybe these semantic differences seem trivial, but I assure you, they are not. Not only does your sense of yourself change with a more powerful vocabulary, so also does your presence in the world. People who display an inner strength are treated differently from those who come across as weak. The more powerfully you speak, the more you will be a force in the world around you.
大家都明白了。开始从你的词汇中排除可怕的，不能做的，问题，挣扎等等。也许这些语义上的差异看起来微不足道，但我向你保证，它们不是。不仅你对自己的感觉会随着词汇量的增加而改变，你在这个世界上的存在也会随之改变。表现出内在力量的人和那些表现出软弱的人会被区别对待。你说话越有力量，你就越能成为你周围世界的一股力量。

As you begin to monitor your vocabulary, you can also bring more power into your life by expanding your comfort zone. What does that mean?
当你开始监控你的词汇量的时候，你也可以通过扩大你的舒适区给你的生活带来更多的力量。这意味着什么？

Most of us operate within a zone that feels right, outside of which we are uncomfortable. For example, we might spend $75 dollars for a pair of shoes, but $100 would make us nervous. We might be willing to initiate friendships with people at the office who are at our level in the company, but would be uncomfortable doing so with one of the higher-ups. We might go to the local deli when eating alone, but would feel really uncomfortable in a luxurious restaurant all by ourselves. We might ask for a $5,000 raise, but $7,000 would make us cringe. We may charge $30 an hour for our services, but we don’t feel that we are worth $35. And so on.
我们大多数人都在一个感觉正确的区域内工作，在这个区域之外我们会感到不舒服。例如，我们可能花75美元买一双鞋，但是100美元会让我们感到紧张。我们可能愿意与办公室里与我们同级别的人建立友谊，但是如果与上级交往，我们会感到不舒服。一个人吃饭的时候，我们可能会去当地的熟食店，但是一个人在豪华的餐厅里会感觉很不舒服。我们可能会要求加薪5000美元，但是7000美元会让我们畏缩。我们可能会为我们的服务收取每小时30美元的费用，但是我们并不认为我们值35美元。以此类推。

For each one of us that zone of comfort is different, but whether we are aware of it or not, all of us—rich or poor, low or high on the totem pole, male or female—make decisions based on the confines of that comfortable space.
对我们每个人来说，舒适区域是不同的，但不管我们是否意识到这一点，我们所有人——无论贫富，无论地位高低，无论男女——都是根据舒适区域的局限性做出决定的。

I suggest that each day you do something that widens that space for you. Call someone you are intimidated to call, buy a pair of shoes that costs more than you would have ever paid in the past, ask for something you want that you have been too frightened to ask for before. Take a risk a day—one small or bold stroke that will make you feel great once you’ve done it. Even if it doesn’t work out the way you wanted it to, at least you’ve tried. You didn’t sit back . . . powerless. Watch what starts to happen when you expand your comfort zone:
我建议你每天都做一些事情来拓宽你的空间。给那些你不敢打电话的人打电话，买一双你以前不会花钱买的鞋子，要一些你以前不敢要的你想要的东西。每天冒一次险——一次小小的或者大胆的尝试，一旦你做到了，你会感觉很棒。即使事情不是你想要的那样，至少你已经尝试过了。你没有袖手旁观，无能为力。观察当你扩展你的舒适区域时会发生什么:

As the drawing shows, with each risk you take, each time you move out of what feels comfortable, you become more powerful. Your whole life expands to take in more of what there is in this world to experience. As your power builds, so does your confidence, so that stretching your comfort zone becomes easier and easier, despite any fear you may be experiencing. The magnitude of the risks you take also expands. In the beginning you may sign up for one evening course after being out of school for fifteen years. Ultimately you may enroll to get your graduate degree. You will be expanding . . . opening up . . . be coming bigger . . . but all at your own pace. As long as you are taking those risks—no matter how small—you are moving yourself to the right on the Pain-to-Power Chart.
如图所示，随着你承担的每一次风险，每一次你离开那些让你感到舒适的地方，你就变得更加强大。你的整个生活都在扩展，吸收更多这个世界上值得体验的东西。随着你的力量的增强，你的自信也在增强，所以尽管你可能正在经历任何恐惧，舒适区的扩展也会变得越来越容易。你所承担的风险也会扩大。刚开始的时候，你可能会在离开学校15年后报名参加一个晚间课程。最终你可能会报名获得研究生学位。你将会扩张，开放，变得更大，但是一切都按照你自己的步伐。只要你敢于承担这些风险——无论风险有多小——你就会让自己在“痛苦与力量”图表上向右移动。

Each night before you go to bed, plan the risk you are going to take the following day. Close your eyes, and in your mind’s eye, practice doing it. Make your visualization as clear as you possibly can. Also, as you go through the day, be aware of where you find yourself hesitating, and start planning your future risks based on these observations. If you can push through the hesitation at the moment you recognize it, great. Remember that the more you expand your comfort zone, the more powerful you become.
每天晚上睡觉前，计划好第二天要承担的风险。闭上你的眼睛，在你的脑海里，练习这样做。让你的想象尽可能清晰。同时，当你度过这一天的时候，意识到你发现自己在犹豫什么，并根据这些观察开始计划你未来的风险。如果你能在意识到这种犹豫的那一刻克服它，那就再好不过了。记住，你越扩展你的舒适区，你就越强大。

PLEASE NOTE: The risks I am talking about do not include physically dangerous acts, such as speeding in a car, or taking drugs. Nor do they include risks that infringe on the rights of other people, such as making a pass at someone’s mate or, for that matter, robbing a bank. Not only could you end up unpopular, dead, or in prison, but you would also be moving yourself far to the left side of the Pain-to-Power Chart. These kinds of acts are not empowering, because they do not have any integrity or love—for one’s self or others—behind them. Without these ingredients, it is impossible to build your sense of self-worth. Hence your ability to handle fear would be greatly diminished.
请注意: 我所说的风险不包括身体上的危险行为，如超速驾驶或吸毒。它们也不包括侵犯他人权利的风险，例如与某人的配偶调情，或者抢劫银行。你不仅可能最终不受欢迎，死亡，或进监狱，而且你也会把自己远远地推到痛苦-权力图表的左边。这些行为并不能赋予你力量，因为它们背后没有任何正直和爱——无论是对自己还是对他人。没有这些因素，就不可能建立你的自我价值感。因此，你处理恐惧的能力将大大减弱。

So take only those risks each day that build your sense of self-worth. These are the risks that enhance your ability to deal with your fears. EXPAND! EXPAND! EXPAND!
因此，每天只承担那些建立自我价值感的风险。这些风险可以增强你处理恐惧的能力。扩大！扩张！扩张！

Whether it feels like it or not, you already have more power than you could ever have imagined. We all have. When I speak of going from pain to power, I am not talking about pulling the power in from any outside source. Inside of you, just waiting to emerge, is an incredible source of energy, which is more than sufficient for you to create a joyful and satisfying life. It isn’t magic. It is only a process of tapping the energy already there, though you are not aware of it.
不管感觉如何，你已经拥有了超乎你想象的力量。我们都有。当我谈到从痛苦走向力量时，我并不是在谈论从任何外部来源获得力量。在你的内心，只是等待出现，是一个令人难以置信的能量来源，这足以让你创造一个快乐和满足的生活。它不是魔法。它只是一个挖掘已经存在的能量的过程，尽管你并没有意识到。

The exercises contained in this book are designed to lead you to this great source of power. Whether you do them or not is a good clue as to whether you are willing at this time to accept all that is within you. If you are not, don’t berate yourself. Just make a commitment to keep working toward it. One way to do this is to read and reread this book and other personal- growth books until you rid yourself of the negative belief systems that are keeping you stuck in your powerlessness. Most of us are filled with old conditioning that is keeping us weak. It takes constant repetition for newer and healthier patterns to take hold.
本书中所包含的练习就是为了引导你找到这个伟大的力量源泉。无论你是否做这些练习，都是一个很好的线索，告诉你你是否愿意在这个时候接受你内在的一切。如果你不是，不要责备自己。只要做出承诺，继续朝着它努力。一种方法是一遍又一遍地阅读这本书和其他关于个人成长的书籍，直到你摆脱了让你陷入无能为力的消极信念系统。我们中的大多数人都充满了让我们变得虚弱的旧习惯。它需要不断重复，才能让更新、更健康的模式站稳脚跟。

You are innately designed to use your personal power. When you don’t, you experience a sense of helplessness, paralysis, and depression—which is your clue that something is not working as it could. You, like all of us, deserve everything that is wonderful and exciting in life. And those feelings emerge only when you get in touch with your powerful self.
你天生就会使用你的个人能力。当你不这样做的时候，你会经历一种无助感，麻痹感和沮丧感——这就是你的线索，某些事情并没有像它可能的那样发挥作用。你，像我们所有人一样，值得拥有生活中一切美好和令人兴奋的东西。这些感觉只有当你接触到强大的自我时才会出现。
